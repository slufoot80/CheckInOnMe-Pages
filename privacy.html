SafePulse Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 5, 2026  |  Last Updated: June 19, 2026

SafePulse ("we", "our", "the app") is a personal safety check-in and emergency alert application developed by Frank Nowicki, and is the app behind this website, checkinonme.app. (The same app is distributed as PulseGuard on the Apple App Store and Amazon Appstore.) This Privacy Policy explains what information SafePulse accesses, how it is used, where it is stored, how long it is retained, and how you can delete it. By using SafePulse you agree to the practices described below.

Summary: SafePulse stores your personal data locally on your own device. We do not collect analytics and we never sell your data. Two features require sending limited information to a secure messaging gateway: (1) confirming that an emergency contact agrees to receive alerts, and (2) on Huawei devices, delivering your emergency SMS. These flows are described in Sections 4 and 5. You can delete the data SafePulse stores on your device at any time by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling the app.

1. Information SafePulse Accesses

SafePulse only accesses information that is required to provide its safety check-in and emergency alert features. Most of the following is stored locally on your device in the app's private database. The limited exceptions — when emergency-contact details are sent to our messaging gateway to confirm consent or to deliver an alert — are described in Sections 4 and 5.

  • Your name (optional) — Used to personalize emergency alert messages sent to your contacts.
  • Emergency contacts — Names and phone numbers you choose to add. You may import these from your phone's contacts (with your permission) or enter them manually.
  • Check-in preferences — Your check-in interval and notification settings.
  • Location data — Your last known GPS coordinates, captured only when you perform a check-in or trigger an SOS alert. Used solely to include a location link in emergency SMS messages.
  • Check-in history — Records of when you completed or missed check-ins.
  • Wellness entries (optional) — Mood entries and journal notes you choose to record inside the app.

What SafePulse does NOT collect

  • No accounts, usernames, passwords, or email registration.
  • No analytics, crash reporting SDKs, or behavioral tracking.
  • No advertising identifiers or third-party advertising SDKs.
  • No background location tracking.
  • No cloud sync or backup of your personal database — SafePulse does not copy your contacts, journal, history, or settings to any server. (The limited contact information used for the opt-in and alert features is covered in Sections 4 and 5.)

2. How SafePulse Uses Your Information

  • Emergency alerts: If you miss a scheduled check-in or activate the SOS button, SafePulse uses your stored emergency contacts and last known location to send SMS alerts. On Google Play and Samsung devices, these are sent directly from your device using Android's SMS system. On Huawei devices, which restrict background SMS, alerts are delivered through our secure messaging gateway (see Sections 4 and 5).
  • SMS content: Alert messages contain your name, a short safety message, and — if location sharing is enabled — a map link to your last known location. SafePulse never sends marketing or promotional SMS.
  • Contact opt-in confirmation: Before SafePulse will ever send alerts to an emergency contact, that person must agree to receive them. When you add a contact, SafePulse registers a one-time invitation with our gateway and you send the contact a link from your own phone. The contact confirms on a web page, and SafePulse checks the gateway when you open the app to learn whether they agreed. A contact who has not agreed never receives any message from our gateway.
  • Phone call on SOS: When you tap the SOS button, SafePulse auto-dials the first emergency contact you saved. No call is made without your explicit SOS action.
  • Local notifications: Used to remind you of upcoming check-ins.

4. Third-Party Services

  • Google Play Services (FusedLocationProvider) — Used to obtain your device's location locally. SafePulse does not transmit your location to Google.
  • Google Maps link — Emergency messages contain a plain Google Maps URL with your coordinates so contacts can view your location. The link is included in the message text; SafePulse itself does not contact Google with this data.
  • Messaging gateway (Cloudflare Workers + Twilio) — To confirm contact opt-in, and to deliver emergency SMS on Huawei devices, SafePulse uses a gateway hosted on Cloudflare Workers that sends SMS through Twilio. The following may be sent to the gateway over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection: your emergency contacts' names and phone numbers, your display name, an anonymous random device identifier (used only to rate-limit the gateway and to match opt-in responses to your app), and — when an alert is delivered through the gateway — the alert message text, including your location map link. No accounts, journal entries, mood data, or check-in history are ever sent to the gateway. See Twilio's privacy policy at twilio.com/legal/privacy and Cloudflare's at cloudflare.com/privacypolicy.

5. Data Storage and Security

  • All personal data you create in the app — contacts, settings, check-in history, journal and mood entries — is stored locally on your device in the app's private storage and database.
  • This local data is never copied to a server. SafePulse cannot access, retrieve, or restore the data held on your device.
  • The only information that leaves your device is what is needed for the contact opt-in and emergency-alert features, sent to our messaging gateway over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection as described in Section 4.
  • Information held by the gateway is transient: opt-in invitation records expire automatically after 30 days, and alert message content is not retained by SafePulse after it is handed to Twilio for delivery (see Section 6).

6. Data Retention

SafePulse retains your data only on your device, and only for as long as you keep the app installed and choose to keep the data. Specifically:

  • Emergency contacts, preferences, and check-in settings are retained until you delete them inside the app, clear the app's storage, or uninstall SafePulse.
  • Check-in history and audit logs are retained on your device until you clear them in app settings, clear the app's storage, or uninstall SafePulse.
  • Mood and journal entries are retained until you delete the individual entries, clear the app's storage, or uninstall SafePulse.
  • Location data is retained as the most recent stored coordinate only; each new check-in overwrites the previous location. It is removed when you clear the app's storage or uninstall SafePulse.
  • Information sent to our messaging gateway is the only data held off your device, and it is transient. Opt-in invitation records — a contact's name and number, your display name, and an anonymous device ID — are stored only to coordinate the contact's consent and expire automatically after 30 days. Emergency-alert message content is passed to Twilio for delivery and is not stored by SafePulse. No other data (journal, mood, history, settings) is ever sent off your device.

7. How to Delete Your Data

You are always in full control of your data and can delete it at any time using any of the following methods:

  1. Delete individual items inside SafePulse — Open SafePulse, go to Contacts, Journal, or Settings, and delete the specific entries you no longer want.
  2. Reset all SafePulse data — Open Android Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Storage → Clear Storage (or Clear Data). This permanently removes the SafePulse database, preferences, contacts, history, and all other locally stored information.
  3. Uninstall SafePulse — Uninstalling the app removes all SafePulse data from your device.

Because SafePulse stores data only on your device and not on any server, performing any of the steps above results in complete deletion of your SafePulse data. There is no separate request to make and nothing for us to delete on our end.

8. Children's Privacy

SafePulse is intended for users aged 18 and over. SafePulse is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided information through SafePulse, please contact us and we will assist with deletion.

9. Your Rights

Because all SafePulse data is stored locally on your device, you have direct, immediate access to it and full control over it:

  • Access: View all of your data inside the app.
  • Correct: Edit any contact, setting, or journal entry in the app.
  • Delete: Use any of the methods listed in Section 7.
  • Withdraw consent: Disable any permission at any time in Android Settings → Apps → SafePulse → Permissions.

10. Changes to This Policy
